Intro

Hello Stranger,
Welcome to my portfolio with couple cycles of my beating heart!

This was taken when I was helping a colleague setting up an MR candy-cane sequence. I have worked with different areas of medical imaging so far that required me to be fluent in the physics of Ultrasound, CT and, last but not the least, my most favorite Magnetic Resonance Imaging - diffusion MRI in particular. Fascination towards these images probably started when I was a toddler. My father being a surgeon used to read these huge books, like "Bailey and Love's Short Practice of Surgery" and other antomy books. I used to smell the pages and tried to understand the images of the book and curiously asked my father if the things I was seeing was inside my body too. Growing up, I often had this calling - How do they do this? Do they cut open humans to image these images? Isn't it really painful? Are these people still alive? It was rational in my immature mind.

So far I have come to know more about these techniques, the physics behind these techniques are intriguing! Everytime I get goosebumps when I think about the physics of MR imaging. So many works, so many contributions and brains have made this technology what it is today. I feel MR is an amazing marvel of our time, even more than we often realize. It is an absolute art in science/physics, that has enabled us to visulize world as a 4D being. .
